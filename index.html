Research and Education

After recovering from the COVID-19 crisis, I developed a deep appreciation for oxygen‚Äîthe essence of life. This inspired me to shift my focus toward trees, nature‚Äôs own oxygen providers. With no background in botany, I began reading extensively about native tree species, their seed cycles, and ecological significance. What started as a personal quest soon grew into a structured effort: I compiled a month-wise seed collection calendar for over 150 native trees. This knowledge, now shared through downloadable resources, is especially helpful for beginners and fellow nature enthusiasts looking to start their green journey.

Partnership and Community

In 2022, I collected around 15 kg of seeds from over 13 native species. These were distributed to more than 50 individuals and plantation groups across Maharashtra, creating a shared ecosystem of growth and awareness. In 2023, my efforts expanded as I visited sacred groves and forested regions around Pune to collect rare and indigenous seeds. These experiences not only deepened my connection with nature but also helped build a community of people passionate about environmental restoration and biodiversity.

Delegation and Advocation

With a renewed sense of purpose, I began work on a native tree nursery, growing over 10,000 saplings. These will be freely distributed during the upcoming monsoon to individuals and groups ready to care for them. By encouraging others to take ownership of these saplings, I hope to create a ripple effect of responsibility and advocacy for the environment. My journey is no longer just personal‚Äîit‚Äôs a call to action for everyone to become stewards of the Earth.
